Praxis II Engineering Portfolio

My name is Nathan Xia, and I am an aspiring aerospace engineer, currently in my first year at the University of Toronto under the engineering science program. Within this design portfolio, I will overview my current approach to engineering design, as well as my utilization of various concepts and tools taught in Praxis I and II courses. These tools have been embedded and integrated throughout my engineering design process, and can be seen in the three projects I will overview: CIV102 Bridge Project, Praxis I Design Report, and Praxis II RFP.

Over the second semester of first year, my approach to engineering design has become much more rigorous and formalized. My stance at the start of the semester has developed from a general framework focused on research to a more formally defined process and philosophy. To me, engineering design is an intelligent, iterative process used to develop solutions subject to constraints and expectations set by stakeholders. This is my formal definition that describes engineering design, which can be found in all of the three engineering design projects featured.

  • – Simulation Verification

From my initial narrowed stance of engineering design at the start of the semester, I have since broadened my perspective and understand the importance of other values within engineering design. These values, such as simplicity, have been implemented in later designs such as Praxis II. I hope that as my design philosophy continues to change as I gain more experience, my capabilities as an engineer will continue to update and improve.
